# Project Brief: Fashion Styling Game

The Fashion Department is looking to develop an interactive styling game to support Dr. Trawick's History of Fashion course. The game challenges players to correctly style outfits for specific historical fashion eras. Players will select clothing items, accessories, and styling details from a curated wardrobe, and receive feedback on the historical accuracy of their choices.

This project presents a unique opportunity: your client is an expert in the subject matter, and your team brings the game design expertise. A successful collaboration depends on both sides communicating clearly and respecting each other's knowledge.

---

## Project Goals

Dr. Trawick wants a game that:

- Tests History of Fashion students on their knowledge of specific historical fashion eras
- Challenges players to assemble historically accurate outfits from a curated set of garments and accessories
- Provides meaningful feedback that teaches — not just evaluates — so students understand why a choice is or isn't period-appropriate
- Covers key eras from the course curriculum (confirmed eras listed below)
- Can be used in a classroom setting as a low-stakes quiz or review activity

This game will have players presented with specific era-related scenarios where the player will need to create an outfit for a specific fashion time period. When the outfit is chosen, the player will confirm their outfit and then be judged successful based on how the outfit fit within specific fashion trends. This game should test the player's understanding of fashion periods and trends.

---

## Fashion Eras to Cover

The game must cover the following eras from the History of Fashion curriculum. Final era list and required depth to be confirmed with the client:

- **1980s** — Changing Roles of Fashion
- **1990s** — Fashion Fragmentation
- **2000s** — New Millennial Trends
- **2010s** — Modern Fashion

Review links provided in [Sources.md](Sources.md) for more details.

---

## Target Audience

- Fashion Design majors enrolled in History of Fashion
- Students with existing foundational knowledge of the covered eras (this is a review and assessment tool, not an introduction to the material)
- Dr. Trawick, as a potential in-class demonstration tool
